# Question Frameworks

Use these categories as a toolkit. Apply the ones that fit the branch; skip the ones that don't. Don't force a category if it produces low-value questions.

## 1. Clarification
Ensure the terms and scope are understood by both parties.
- "What exactly do you mean by X?"
- "What is the boundary of X — what is in scope and what is out?"
- "Can you restate the goal in one sentence?"

## 2. Assumption-probing
Surface what is being taken for granted.
- "What must be true for X to hold?"
- "What are you assuming about [the user, the system, the timeline, the data]?"
- "Has this been true before? Under what conditions?"

## 3. Evidence-testing
Check whether claims are grounded or speculative.
- "How would you know if X were false?"
- "What data or observation would change your mind?"
- "Have you verified this, or is it a hypothesis?"

## 4. Implication-tracing
Follow consequences to find hidden dependencies or conflicts.
- "If X happens, then what happens to Y?"
- "What breaks if X succeeds? What breaks if X fails?"
- "Who or what else is affected by this decision?"

## 5. Counter-example / falsification
Test the robustness of the plan against edge cases.
- "Can you think of a case where this approach fails?"
- "What is the simplest scenario that would invalidate this?"
- "What is the worst plausible outcome?"

## 6. Alternative exploration
Ensure the space of options has been considered.
- "What other ways to achieve the same goal have you ruled out?"
- "Why is this approach better than doing nothing?"
- "What would a skeptic recommend instead?"
